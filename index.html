The concept behind this is to create a digital d20 table inspired by the ones from Stargrave but also featuring more random effects, both positive and negative. Crew members and hostiles have a slim chance to buff themselves, heal or gain actions, while possibly also spawning more loot or more enemies!
VaranStone
VaranStone is a valuable mutagenic resource found across the mortal realms. Wherever it erupts from the ground, armies of all factions dash to claim it. Either to bolster their own forces, for trade, or just to keep it out of the enemy's clutches. Can you claim it before the enemy does?
Deployment: Choose a deployment by rolling a dice and comparing the result against the deployments table on page 294 of the main Age of Sigmar rulebook. This can be played on any size table.
Setting Up the Battlefield: The center of the battlefield should be devoid of terrain but it can be placed anywhere else and in any quantities desired.
Placing VaranStones: Each player takes turns placing 6 dice into their hand and then dropping them from 12 inches up onto the center of the battlefield. Where these dice land are your VaranStones, with the value facing up being how hard they are to mine.
Special Actions: At any time during the battle, when a unit is within 3 inches of a VaranStone, they may choose to "mine" it. Spend a command point and reduce the number on the VaranStone dice by 1. Once the dice reaches 1, the VaranStone is fully "mined", controlled by the unit who did the final mining, and is now being carried by that unit. If this unit is destroyed, they drop the VaranStone and another unit can pick it up.
Victory Condition: At the end of the 5th battleround, control more VaranStone than your opponent for a major victory, control an equal number for a minor victory.
This house rule is based off the core mechanic of "Moonstone". In Moonstone, Jim Hensen-like creatures battle it out to collect the titular resource of... moonstone. The way the objectives are placed creates a focus point for both players emanating from the center of the board. It also will create choices with players either using their command points to buff their units or claim the resources.
